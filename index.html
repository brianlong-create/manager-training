Block 1

Tactical Management & Leadership

Core frameworks from Simon Sinek and Leila Hormozi. Watch, discuss, apply.

1. Start With Why

Simon Sinek • The Golden Circle
1

The Golden Circle: WHY (purpose) → HOW (process) → WHAT (product). Most organizations communicate outside-in. Great leaders communicate inside-out.

2

People don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it. This applies to your team too.

3

Your WHY is your filter. Use it to make decisions, hire, and say no to things that don't align.

Discussion Question

"What's the 'why' behind your team's work right now? Not the what — the why."

2. Leader vs Manager

Distinguishing the two roles
1

Managers handle complexity. Planning, budgeting, organizing. It's about order.

2

Leaders handle change. Setting direction, aligning people, motivating. It's about movement.

3

You need both. Know which hat you're wearing in each moment.

Paired Exercise (10 min)

Each manager lists 3 things they did last week. Categorize each as "managing" or "leading."

Group Debrief

"Where are you over-indexed on managing vs leading?"

3. Circle of Safety

Simon Sinek • Building Trust
1

Leaders set the circle. When people feel safe, they focus outward on threats and opportunities.

2

Trust is earned in drops and lost in buckets. Every interaction builds or erodes the circle.

3

Vulnerability is the entry fee. You go first. Model ownership and honesty about mistakes.

Vulnerable Discussion (8 min)

"Name a moment you broke the circle of safety on your team. What did you do about it?"

4. Skill × Will Matrix

Assessing & developing your team
1

High Skill / High Will: Delegate and empower. Don't micromanage your stars.

2

High Skill / Low Will: Motivate and re-engage. Often a leadership problem.

3

Low Skill / High Will: Guide and train. Diamonds in the rough. Invest heavily.

4

Low Skill / Low Will: Direct conversation. Clear expectations, timelines, consequences.

Private Exercise (10 min)

Plot your direct reports on the matrix. Then discuss: "What's your move for each quadrant?"

5. Radical Accountability

Owning outcomes, not excuses
1

Accountability starts at the top. If your team missed the target, YOU missed it.

2

The opposite of accountability is blame. Blame travels down, accountability travels up.

3

Public praise, private correction. Celebrate wins loudly. Address misses one-on-one.

Discussion Question

"What's a recent miss where you took accountability vs delegated blame?"

6. The $100/Hour Test

Leila Hormozi • Time Management for Leaders
1

Score every task: $10/hr, $100/hr, $1,000/hr, $10,000/hr.

2

$10/hr: Admin, data entry, scheduling. DELEGATE IT.

3

$100/hr: Problem solving, team coaching, process improvement. Your core job.

4

$1,000+/hr: Strategy, relationship building, innovation. This is where leaders win.

Exercise (10 min)

List your last 5 work activities. Score each. Identify what to delegate this week.

7. The Infinite Game

Simon Sinek • Long-term thinking
1

Finite games have winners and losers. Business is infinite — there's no final "win."

2

Play to outlast, not to beat. Build something that sustains.

3

Worthy rivals, not competitors. Learn from others who do things well.

Discussion Question

"Where are we playing a finite game when we should be playing infinite?"

HR Essentials

Buzzwords & Situations

Know the terms. Know when to call HR. You don't need to be HR — you need to know when to call them.

HR Buzzwords — Click to Reveal

At-Will Employment
Click to reveal
Either party can end the relationship at any time, for any legal reason. Doesn't mean you can fire for illegal reasons.
Constructive Feedback
Click to reveal
Feedback focused on specific behaviors and impact. Not "you're bad" but "when you do X, the impact is Y, suggest Z."
Progressive Discipline
Click to reveal
Stepped approach: verbal warning → written warning → final warning → termination. Document every step.
Hostile Work Environment
Click to reveal
Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristic, severe or pervasive enough to create intimidating environment.
Reasonable Accommodation
Click to reveal
Modification enabling a qualified person with a disability to perform essential functions. Interactive process with HR.
Documentation
Click to reveal
Written records of issues, conversations, warnings. If it wasn't documented, it didn't happen. Date it, be specific.
Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
Click to reveal
Formal, time-bound document with specific gaps, expected improvements, support, and consequences. 30-90 days.
Protected Class
Click to reveal
Groups protected by law: race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic info, veteran status.
Retaliation
Click to reveal
Punishing an employee for protected activity. #1 EEOC charge. Watch for subtle forms.
Disparate Impact
Click to reveal
A neutral policy that disproportionately affects a protected group. Intent doesn't matter — impact does.
FMLA
Click to reveal
Up to 12 weeks unpaid, job-protected leave per year. Don't ask medical details — route to HR.
Undue Hardship
Click to reveal
Threshold for denying accommodation. High bar — most accommodations are low/no cost. Engage HR before denying.

Tabletop Scenarios — What Would You Do?

"You don't need to be HR — you need to know when to call HR."

⚠️ Scenario 1: The Complaint
A direct report tells you a peer made an off-color joke about their religion. What do you do?

The Right Move

Thank them. Don't dismiss or investigate yourself. Document what they said. Contact HR within 24 hours. Don't promise confidentiality you can't guarantee.

⚠️ Scenario 2: The Accommodation
A team member discloses ADHD and asks for flexible schedule. What's appropriate to ask?

The Right Move

CAN ask: what schedule changes help, essential functions, coverage. CANNOT ask: diagnosis details, medication, treatment. Route to HR for interactive process.

⚠️ Scenario 3: The Performance Issue
You've coached someone for 3 months with no improvement. What's next?

The Right Move

Move to formal documentation. Write specific gaps with examples. Contact HR about a PIP with measurable goals and clear timeline.

⚠️ Scenario 4: The "Off the Record"
A report says "Can I tell you something off the record?" before sharing concerns. How do you respond?

The Right Move

Be honest BEFORE they share: "I can't promise off the record if it involves harassment, discrimination, or safety." Then listen. Never promise what you can't deliver.

⚠️ Scenario 5: The Escalation Call
When do you loop in HR vs handle it yourself?

The Decision Framework

Handle yourself: Routine coaching, scheduling, workload, skill development.

Loop in HR: Discrimination, harassment, retaliation, ADA, FMLA, threats, legal claims, termination.

Rule of thumb: "When in doubt, document and call HR."
